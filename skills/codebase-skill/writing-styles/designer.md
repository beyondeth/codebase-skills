# === STYLE OVERVIEW ===

## When to Use Designer Style

Use this style for design writing that explains context, constraints, exploration, rationale, and impact in a persuasive case-study format.

### Perfect For
- UX case studies
- Product design retrospectives
- Interaction or information architecture decisions
- Portfolio-quality writeups
- Design-system or workflow reasoning posts

# === TARGET READER ===

- Product designers
- PMs and engineers evaluating design decisions
- Hiring managers or peers reading a case study
- Cross-functional teammates who need design rationale

# === OUTPUT GOAL ===

The reader should understand:

- what problem space existed
- what constraint shaped the work
- what alternatives were explored
- why one direction won
- what changed for users or the business

# === CORE DESIGNER PRINCIPLES ===

## 1. Context First

Never begin with a polished solution. Begin with the state of the product, user pain, and design constraint.

## 2. Show Rationale, Not Just Taste

Explain why a design choice worked in relation to comprehension, friction, speed, trust, or task completion.

## 3. Alternatives Matter

A strong case study shows that other paths were considered and intentionally rejected.

## 4. Impact is Part of the Design Story

Design writing should connect choices to outcomes, not stop at mockups.

## 5. Keep the Narrative Concrete

Use moments, screens, flows, or before/after states rather than generic claims like “the UX improved.”

# === WRITING GUIDELINES ===

## Recommended Structure

```markdown
## Product Context
What was the environment, audience, and job to be done?

## Core Problem
Where was friction, confusion, or drop-off happening?

## Constraints
What limited the solution? Time, tech, org, or platform?

## Exploration
What directions were considered?

## Chosen Direction
What did you select and why?

## Before / After
What changed in the flow, hierarchy, or interaction?

## Impact
What improved for users or the business?

## What I Would Refine Next
What remains unresolved?
```

# === QUALITY ENHANCEMENT GUIDE ===

## Designer Upgrade Patterns

- Convert visual taste language into usability language
- Add constraints explicitly
- Add one rejected alternative
- Add one concrete user impact or business impact statement

# === ENHANCEMENT TECHNIQUES ===

## Before -> Friction -> Change -> Result

Use this structure to keep the case study persuasive without becoming decorative.

## Replace Taste Words

- `더 예쁘게` → `스캔 속도를 높이기 위해`
- `정돈했다` → `우선순위가 보이도록 계층을 재배치했다`

# === QUALITY CHECKLIST ===

- The post contains context and constraints
- At least one rejected direction is visible
- The chosen solution has rationale, not just description
- The before/after difference is concrete
- The ending acknowledges next refinements or remaining risks
