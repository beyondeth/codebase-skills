# === STYLE OVERVIEW ===

## When to Use Marketer Style

Use this style for growth and performance marketing writing that persuades with hypotheses, experiments, metrics, and next actions.

### Perfect For
- Channel experiments and growth learnings
- Landing page or copy iteration reviews
- Campaign retrospectives
- Acquisition or activation analysis
- Messaging and positioning lessons grounded in results

# === TARGET READER ===

- Growth marketers and performance marketers
- Founders and product teams responsible for acquisition
- Operators looking for repeatable playbooks
- Readers who care about conversion, retention, or signal quality

# === OUTPUT GOAL ===

The reader should understand:

- what hypothesis was tested
- who the audience was
- what changed in copy, channel, or offer
- what numbers moved
- what should be repeated or discarded next

# === CORE MARKETER PRINCIPLES ===

## 1. Lead with the Hypothesis

State the bet up front. Do not bury the assumption behind a long setup.

## 2. Metrics Need Context

Raw numbers are not enough. Explain baseline, time window, and channel context.

## 3. Translate Tactics into Learnings

Do not stop at “it worked.” Explain why it likely worked or failed.

## 4. Keep the Reader Moving

Every section should help the reader decide what to test next.

## 5. Prefer Specific Audience Language

The post should sound like it knows exactly who the message was written for.

# === WRITING GUIDELINES ===

## Recommended Structure

```markdown
## Hypothesis
What did we believe would change and for whom?

## Audience and Context
What segment, channel, and moment did this apply to?

## What We Changed
Offer, copy, CTA, timing, funnel step, or channel mix

## Results
Baseline vs after, with real numbers

## Why It Likely Worked / Failed
Interpret the signal, not just the metric

## Repeatable Playbook
What should another team copy or adapt?

## Next Test
What should happen next?
```

# === QUALITY ENHANCEMENT GUIDE ===

## Marketer Upgrade Patterns

- Add a visible baseline before showing a lift
- Name the audience explicitly
- Separate result from interpretation
- End with a next experiment, not a vague conclusion

# === ENHANCEMENT TECHNIQUES ===

## Baseline -> Change -> Delta -> Interpretation

Use this pattern for experiment-heavy posts.

## Replace Empty Success Language

- `반응이 좋았다` → `댓글 저장률이 1.4배 높아졌다`
- `전환이 개선됐다` → `가입 전환율이 2.1%에서 3.4%로 상승했다`

# === QUALITY CHECKLIST ===

- The hypothesis is visible near the top
- The audience and channel are named
- Results include baseline and delta
- The interpretation is separated from the raw metric
- The ending proposes a next experiment or scaling rule
