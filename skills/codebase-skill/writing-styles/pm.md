# === STYLE OVERVIEW ===

## When to Use PM Style

Use this style for product writing that must persuade through user insight, decision rationale, and business impact.

### Perfect For
- Product strategy notes and product essays
- Roadmap rationale and prioritization stories
- Feature retrospectives and launch reviews
- User problem framing and opportunity analysis
- Alignment posts for cross-functional stakeholders

# === TARGET READER ===

- Product managers
- Founders and leadership
- Designers and engineers who need product context
- Operators who need the why behind a decision

# === OUTPUT GOAL ===

The reader should leave understanding:

- what problem mattered
- what options existed
- why this decision was chosen
- what trade-off was accepted
- what the result implies for future work

# === CORE PM PRINCIPLES ===

## 1. Start with the Problem, Not the Feature

Open with the user or business tension before naming the solution.

## 2. Make Trade-offs Visible

Do not present decisions as inevitable. Show what was rejected and why.

## 3. Tie Insight to Action

User feedback only matters when it clearly changes prioritization, scope, or sequencing.

## 4. Write for Alignment

The post should reduce disagreement by making assumptions, constraints, and success criteria explicit.

## 5. End with a Decision Rule

Close with a reusable heuristic, not a generic summary.

# === WRITING GUIDELINES ===

## Recommended Structure

```markdown
## Problem Signal
What user or business issue created urgency?

## Why This Matters Now
What changed in timing, scale, or risk?

## Options We Considered
What paths existed and what did each cost?

## Decision and Rationale
What did we choose and why?

## Trade-offs
What did we accept or postpone?

## Outcome / What We Learned
What happened after the decision?

## Decision Rules
What should teams reuse next time?
```

# === QUALITY ENHANCEMENT GUIDE ===

## PM Upgrade Patterns

- Replace feature description with problem framing
- Replace opinion-only language with user signal or business evidence
- Add one explicit trade-off section
- Add one sentence for stakeholder impact

# === ENHANCEMENT TECHNIQUES ===

## Problem -> Choice -> Trade-off -> Outcome

This is the base rhythm for most PM posts.

## Use Simple Comparison Tables

```markdown
| Option | 장점 | 비용/리스크 | 선택 여부 |
```

# === QUALITY CHECKLIST ===

- The post starts with a problem signal
- At least two options or rejected paths are visible
- The trade-off is explicit
- The decision is tied to user or business evidence
- The ending gives a reusable product heuristic
